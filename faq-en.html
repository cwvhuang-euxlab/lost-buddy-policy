Lost Buddy FAQ

Lost Buddy helps connect lost dogs with people who can help. Owners can post lost dog information, while others can share sightings to provide clues.

Below are common questions and answers to help you better understand how to use Lost Buddy.

Lost Dogs

The most crucial details are recent, clear photos (showing distinct facial features, body proportions, and recognizable markings), the time of disappearance, and the last known location.

In addition to completing all required fields, we highly recommend describing your dog's personality and tips for approaching them (e.g., highly motivated by food, friendly, or easily startled/wary). These details help well-meaning people approach your pet correctly without scaring them away.

Dog Sightings

Please sign up or log in to your account and tap "Sighting". Upload a photo, indicate the coat color and breed, and fill in the current time, location, and characteristics. Once submitted, owners can instantly cross-reference this vital clue through filter criteria.

App Usage & Features

You can browse lost and sighting posts without signing in. To create or update a post, you need to register and sign in so we can help maintain reliable information and allow you to manage your posts.

You can upload a maximum of 4 photos per post. We suggest picking shots that best represent your dog and effectively display its identifying traits.

If the upload feels slow, it might be due to a weak network signal or because our system is optimizing the image resolution in the background. Try moving to an area with a stronger signal.

To maximize exposure for lost pets, the App currently allows browsing without logging in. We take your privacy and safety very seriously; the platform only requires a nickname. Phone numbers and social links are entirely optional—you can choose whether to provide them.

Safety Tip: If you do leave a search group link, we recommend verifying the identity and intent of anyone who reaches out before sharing more personal contact details.

Notifications are not available in Lost Buddy yet.

We plan to add notifications in the future to help users stay updated on matching lost dog posts, nearby sightings, and updates to followed cases.

About Lost Buddy

Lost Buddy was created by UX designer ChihWei and UI designer Shen.

We found that lost dogs and the people searching for them often miss each other because information is scattered across different places:

  • Information is spread across different platforms and communities, making it difficult to keep track of updates.
  • Important clues can easily get buried under newer posts.
  • Without an effective way to compare details, people must manually check photos, characteristics, and locations one by one.

Therefore, we aim to make lost dog information and sightings easier to connect through better organization, filtering, and presentation, helping more dogs find their way home.

Core features for lost dog posts and sighting reports will remain free.

We do not want to interrupt the critical moments when owners are searching for their dogs and people are sharing helpful sightings with intrusive ads. Lost Buddy is currently developed and maintained by the designers themselves. If operating costs increase in the future, we may explore voluntary support options to help keep Lost Buddy available.

If you find Lost Buddy helpful, please consider sharing it with your community, leaving a 5-star rating, or writing a review. Your support means a lot to us as independent developers.

Lost Buddy is still growing, and some features are limited by available resources, such as real-time notifications, area-based alerts, support for more photos and videos, and sharing recovery experiences.

The most meaningful support is simply keeping an eye out for dogs that may be lost and helping report sightings when you can. Your small act of kindness could become the clue that helps a dog find its way home.
